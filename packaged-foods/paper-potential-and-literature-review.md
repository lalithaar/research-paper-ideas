**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Frame as a **"conceptual framework paper"** that sets the research agenda

### Challenge 2: "The technical implementation is not detailed enough"

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Add an **"Implementation Roadmap"** section with:
  - Specific ML architectures (e.g., Graph Neural Networks for ingredient networks)

  - Phased implementation plan (pilot ‚Üí scale)

- Provide **complexity analysis** (computational feasibility)

### Challenge 3: "How is this different from existing fraud detection systems?"

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- **Clearly distinguish** between:
  - **Fraud detection** (illegal mislabeling) ‚Üê existing work
  - **Regulatory evasion detection** (legal but harmful reformulation) ‚Üê your novel contribution
- Emphasize that NPE is **legal but unethical**, requiring a different regulatory response
- Show how your system detects **patterns** rather than individual violations

### Challenge 4: "Will regulators actually adopt this?"

**Mitigation Strategy:**
- Include a **"Policy Translation"** section addressing:
  - Legal feasibility (automated vs. semi-automated regulation)
  - Stakeholder buy-in (industry, consumer groups, regulators)
  - Governance safeguards (human oversight, transparency, appeals)
- Propose a **pilot program** with FSSAI
- Cite examples of algorithmic regulation in other domains (e.g., algorithmic trading oversight)
